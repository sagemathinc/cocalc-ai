## The one promise

**Make computational work easier to share, review, and continue.**

Lead with this. It is the headline idea. Workspace, AI, and deploy-anywhere are *proof of
this promise* — they are never the headline.

## Who the page is for (in priority order)

1. **Researchers & scientists** (the user). Question they arrive with:
   *"Will this fit how my team actually works — and can we pick up each other's work?"*
2. **Executive / platform decision-makers** (the buyer). Question they arrive with:
   *"Which operating model and price fits us, and can we run it our way?"*

Both must feel spoken to within the first screen. The page routes #1 to workflow proof and
#2 to operating-model choice — without becoming two separate pages.

## The page's job

State the promise → prove it → route to the right operating model → drive ONE action.

## Proof spine (the only three things we prove)

1. **Continuity** — work lives in one shared project; you can hand it off and continue it.
2. **Reviewable & recoverable** — technical work can be inspected before handoff and
   recovered in practice.
3. **Deploy anywhere** — the same workspace runs hosted (CoCalc.ai), local (Plus),
   single-VM (Star), private team (Launchpad), or self-operated enterprise (Rocket).

If a section doesn't advance one of these three, it doesn't belong on the home page.

## The one primary CTA

**Start on CoCalc.ai** (sign up). This is the single obvious next step, repeated at most
twice (hero + close). Everything else is demoted:
- Secondary: **Compare operating models** (for the decision-maker).
- Tertiary: feature/product links — present, never competing for the eye.

## Hard "do nots" (things you've already flagged)

- No tool-inventory headlines ("notebooks, code, documents, compute, AI…").
- Don't repeat the same idea across sections. Each section earns its place once.
- No "serious technical work" or other tacky/exclusive phrasing.
- No invented metrics, customer proof, or benchmark numbers (see AGENTS.md hygiene rule).
- No autonomous open-ended "iterate" runs on this page.

## Done means

- A reader gets the promise in one sentence, above the fold.
- Researcher and decision-maker each find their path within the first screen.
- The page reads as **one argument with one obvious next step** — no idea repeats.
- It looks **intentionally designed**, not assembled: consistent rhythm, type, and color.
- A single word can be changed in under a minute without editing a test.
- The team is proud to ship it.
